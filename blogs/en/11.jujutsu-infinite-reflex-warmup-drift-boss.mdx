## The First-Match Cold Start Is Real

You sit down, load into Jujutsu Infinite, and your first two or three duels are rough — mistimed dodges, dropped combos, reads you'd normally nail. Then suddenly you're warmed up and playing fine. Sound familiar? That cold-start slump is real, and it's costing you matches (and rank) while your reflexes spin up.

Athletes warm up before they compete. Gamers mostly don't — we just throw ourselves into ranked and hope match three is when we click. A quick, timing-focused mini-game fixes that: 60–90 seconds to prime your reactions *before* it counts.

## Why Timing Games Specifically

We've talked before about [daily puzzles for the downtime](/blog/jujutsu-infinite-daily-brain-puzzles) — those train focus and pattern recognition. But a reflex warmup is a different tool for a different problem. Where a cryptic puzzle wakes up your *brain*, a one-tap timing game wakes up your *hands*:

- **Reaction time.** Pure tap-at-the-right-moment gameplay drills the exact reflex you use to dodge an incoming attack.
- **Timing precision.** Hitting a narrow window over and over calibrates your sense of *when*, not just *what* — the difference between a clean parry and eating a combo.
- **Flow state, fast.** A simple, escalating challenge pulls you into focus in under a minute, so you arrive at your first duel already locked in.
- **Zero setup.** No download, no account — open a tab, play, close it, queue up.
