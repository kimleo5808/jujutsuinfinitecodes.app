## That Clip Deserves to Be Seen

You landed the perfect cursed technique combo, pulled a rare clan, or clutched a boss with 1 HP — and then it vanished into the void because you never recorded it. Sharing your best Jujutsu Infinite moments is half the fun, and it's also how channels and Discord communities grow.

The problem is the pipeline between "I have footage" and "it's posted and looks good" trips up a lot of players. File formats don't match, clips are too big to upload, or the quality gets crushed. Here's how to do it cleanly, on PC and mobile.

## Step 1: Record the Clip

**On PC:**
- **Windows Game Bar** (`Win + G`) records the active window with no extra software. Good enough for quick clips.
- **OBS Studio** (free) gives you far more control — bitrate, resolution, and instant-replay-style clip saving. It records to MKV or MOV by default, which matters in a minute.
- **NVIDIA ShadowPlay / AMD ReLive** let you save the last 30 seconds after something cool happens, which is perfect for highlights you didn't plan.

**On mobile:**
- iOS and Android both have a built-in screen recorder in the control center. Enable it before you start a session so you're ready when the moment hits.

## Step 2: Convert to the Right Format

This is the step most people miss. Your recording software and the platform you're posting to rarely speak the same language:

- **OBS records MKV/MOV**, but TikTok, Discord, and most editors want **MP4**.
- **Mobile recordings** can be huge — too big for Discord's upload limit or slow to send.
- **Audio-only highlights** (a funny voice-chat moment) need to be pulled out as MP3.

You don't need heavy desktop software for this. A browser-based converter like [Filevo](https://filevo.app/) handles it without an install or signup — upload your MKV or MOV, convert to MP4, and download. It also does audio (MP3, WAV) and images (PNG → WebP/JPG), deletes your files automatically within 24 hours, and adds no watermark, which matters when you're posting publicly.

Common conversions for game clips:

| You have | You want | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| MKV / MOV (OBS) | MP4 | Universal upload, smaller size |
| Large MP4 | Compressed MP4 | Fit Discord/TikTok limits |
| Video clip | MP3 | Share a funny audio moment |
| Screenshot PNG | WebP / JPG | Faster to post, smaller file |

## Step 3: Trim and Polish

- Cut to the moment. A 12-second clip of the combo beats a 90-second clip with 78 seconds of walking.
- For vertical platforms (TikTok, Shorts), crop to 9:16 so the action fills the screen.
- Add a one-line text overlay for context: "Rare clan first try 😤"

## Step 4: Post Where Your Audience Is

- **TikTok / YouTube Shorts** — best reach for short, punchy highlights. Add trending sounds and a hook in the caption.
- **Discord** — your community's home base. Keep files under the server's upload limit (convert/compress first).
- **Reddit / X** — great for "look what I pulled" moments and build discussion.

Whatever platform, link your audience to something useful in the caption or comments — like the latest [Jujutsu Infinite codes](/) so new viewers have a reason to follow you.

## Quick Troubleshooting

- **"My clip won't upload to Discord."** It's over the size limit — compress it or convert to a smaller MP4 with [Filevo](https://filevo.app/).
- **"The video plays on my PC but not my phone."** It's probably an MKV — convert to MP4.
- **"Quality looks terrible after posting."** Record at a higher bitrate and avoid re-compressing the same file multiple times.

## Wrapping Up

Recording is easy. The reason most great Jujutsu Infinite clips never get shared is the messy middle step — wrong format, too big, won't upload. Sort out a simple capture-convert-post routine once, and you'll be sharing highlights in minutes instead of giving up halfway. Now go stock up on the latest codes, jump in, and catch your next clutch moment on camera.
