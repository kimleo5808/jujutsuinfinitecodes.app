## What AI Video Actually Unlocks

Modern AI video tools turn a text prompt or a single image into a short clip. For a gaming creator, that covers a surprising amount of what you'd otherwise pay an editor for:

- **Scroll-stopping hooks.** The first 3 seconds of a Short or TikTok — the part that decides whether anyone watches the rest.
- **Looping backgrounds.** Motion behind your static guides, tier lists, or code roundups so they don't feel like a slideshow.
- **Transitions and B-roll.** Energy-charged cutaways between gameplay segments.

## A Simple Workflow

Here's how to fit AI video into a Jujutsu Infinite content pipeline without overcomplicating it:

1. **Start from an image you already have.** Took a great thumbnail or generated key art? Use image-to-video to bring it to life with subtle motion — drifting energy, a slow zoom, particles.
2. **Or prompt from scratch.** Describe the vibe: "dark anime battlefield, swirling purple cursed energy, dramatic camera push-in, cinematic, looping."
3. **Keep it short.** Intros and hooks should be 3–5 seconds. Short AI clips also generate faster and look cleaner.
4. **Stitch it in.** Drop the clip into your free editor (CapCut, DaVinci Resolve) as an intro, a hook, or a background layer behind your text.

Prompt tips that work for game content:

- **Lead with mood and motion:** "slow push-in," "energy swirling," "rim light flicker" — verbs matter more than nouns.
- **Specify orientation:** "9:16 vertical for Shorts" or "16:9 cinematic" so it fits your platform.
- **Generate a batch, pick the best.** AI video is iterative — make several short variations and keep the strongest.

> Keep it original. Generate stylized effects, energy, and abstract backdrops — don't try to recreate copyrighted characters or official art. Original motion is safer for monetization and still looks the part.
