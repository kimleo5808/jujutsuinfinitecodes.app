## What's a Solar Return Chart?

Every year, around your birthday, the Sun returns to the exact spot it occupied the moment you were born. The chart cast for that precise instant is your **solar return** — a snapshot that astrology fans read as the "theme" of your next twelve months.

Unlike your birth chart (fixed for life), the solar return resets annually. So while your sign tells you *how* you play, your solar return is a fun framework for *when* the next year might favor grinding, competing, or chilling.

You can generate one in seconds with [ChartNova](https://chartnova.app/) — a free solar return chart calculator that adds an AI reading for love, career, and life direction. No signup, just your birth date, time, and city. We're going to borrow that "year ahead" framing and map it onto your Jujutsu Infinite calendar.

## Turning Your Chart into a Gaming Plan (For Fun)

Here's a light-hearted way to read your solar return like a gamer:

- **A "fresh start" energy year?** Perfect time to reroll, try a new clan, or main a fighting style you've never touched. Lean into experimentation.
- **A "focus and discipline" year?** Set a real goal — hit a rank, complete a hard grind, build the perfect build. This is your lock-in season.
- **A "social and connection" year?** Prioritize duos, clans, and community events. Find your [perfect partner](/blog/jujutsu-infinite-duo-zodiac-compatibility) and run content together.
- **A "rest and reset" year?** Permission to play casually. Log in for fun, not for the leaderboard, and avoid burnout.

> None of this is destiny — it's a prompt. The real value is being *intentional* about how you spend your gaming time instead of grinding on autopilot until you're bored of the game.

## A Simple Birthday Ritual

Make it an annual thing. Around your birthday each year:

1. **Generate your solar return chart** with [ChartNova](https://chartnova.app/) and read the AI summary of your year's themes.
2. **Pick one gaming intention** that matches the vibe — a rank goal, a new style to learn, a community to join, or simply "play less, enjoy more."
3. **Write it down** somewhere you'll see it — your notes app, your Discord status, wherever.
4. **Check in mid-year** and adjust. Did the "focus" year actually push you to that rank? Did the "rest" year save you from burnout?

It's a fun, zero-pressure way to give your gaming year a little structure — and a great conversation starter for your clan.
